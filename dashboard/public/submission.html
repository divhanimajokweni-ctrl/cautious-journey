6-Page Technical Disclosure

PROOFBRIDGE LINER — THE TRUST LAYER FOR VERIFIABLE AI · V1.0 · MAY 2026 · MIHLE "DIVHANI" MAJOKWENI · VAGUELY VANITY LLC

Abstract

ProofBridge Liner is a Bayesian safety kernel that converts every AI output into a provable, adjudicatable statement. The kernel applies a Beta-Binomial posterior belief check calibrated against an industry-specific risk parameter γ, emitting a live SAFE / TRIP verdict together with a deterministic reasoning chain and a cryptographically signed proof trace. A TRIP verdict is enforced on-chain by an EVM circuit-breaker smart contract. The integrity gap — deed valid on-chain while title is disputed off-chain — is the structural vulnerability this system is designed to eliminate.

Problem Statement

1.1 The Integrity Gap. A deed hash can be valid on-chain while the underlying property title is disputed, encumbered, or fraudulent. On-chain attestation is binary — no model of accumulated evidence, no Safety Margin, no audit trail a regulator can read.

1.2 Why Bayesian Inference. A Beta-Binomial model is the natural fit. Each deed verification event is a Bernoulli trial. The Beta distribution is the conjugate prior — posterior updates in closed form with no retraining and no latency overhead. α=24, β=8 means 24 clean verifications and 8 anomalies. Self-interpreting at the point of decision.

The Mathematics

μ = (α + 1) / (α + β + 2)  — Posterior belief
τ = τ₀ / (1 + γ · β/α)   — Calibrated threshold
S = μ − τ  →  SAFE iff S > 0  (primary interpretability anchor)

Limitations & Risk Disclosure

Five classes disclosed in full: prior quality dependency, evidence quality, calibration drift, adversarial α inflation, regulatory scope. We hold that disclosed limitations increase institutional trust; undisclosed limitations destroy it.
