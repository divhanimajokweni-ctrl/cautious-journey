The Problem

AI output is unverifiable by default

AI systems generate outputs that are easy to fabricate, nearly impossible to verify, and impossible to audit or enforce.

"AI said so" is not a verification strategy.

The Solution

A Bayesian safety kernel with on-chain enforcement

 User / AI Output
       │
       ▼
 ┌─────────────┐
 │   AI Model  │
 └──────┬──────┘
        │
        ▼
①  TEE Gate     — hardware attestation
②  Bayesian μ   — belief check
③  Threshold τ  — γ-adjusted
④  Verdict      — SAFE or TRIP
⑤  HMAC trace   — signed & timestamped

Two-part guarantee:

Off-chain — every verdict ships with a reasoning chain, belief μ, threshold τ, and safety margin S = μ − τ.

On-chain — a TRIP immediately halts transfers via the CircuitBreaker EVM smart contract.

Verdict = SAFE iff μ > τ  |  S = μ − τ

The Math

Industry-calibrated Bayesian reasoning

Latent risk:  θ ~ Beta(α, β)
Posterior μ: (α + 1) / (α + β + 2)
Threshold τ: τ₀ / (1 + γ · β/α)
Verdict:      SAFE iff μ > τ
